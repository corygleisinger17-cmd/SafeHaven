# SafeHaven Product Roadmap

> **Version:** 1.0  
> **Status:** Strategic  
> **Audience:** Product, Engineering, Leadership  
> **Last Updated:** July 2026

---

# Purpose

The purpose of this roadmap is not to define every feature SafeHaven will ever include.

Its purpose is to describe how SafeHaven evolves from a simple household inventory into the world's most intelligent home ownership platform.

Each phase builds on the previous one.

Nothing in later phases should compromise the stability or trust established in earlier phases.

---

# Guiding Principle

SafeHaven should never add intelligence before it has trustworthy information.

The roadmap intentionally follows this progression:

```
Trust
    ↓
Organization
    ↓
Understanding
    ↓
Guidance
    ↓
Automation
    ↓
Intelligence
```

Every release should strengthen this foundation.

# Phase 2 — Ownership Understanding

## Objective

Transform inventory into connected knowledge.

SafeHaven should begin understanding relationships rather than simply storing records.

---

## Capabilities

- Compatible accessories
- Replacement parts
- Consumables
- Battery platforms
- Fuel requirements
- Manufacturer relationships
- Related assets
- Expected lifespan
- Warranty defaults
- Maintenance templates

---

## Example

Instead of knowing:

> "This is a DeWalt Drill"

SafeHaven understands:

- Uses 20V MAX batteries
- User owns three compatible batteries
- Charger is stored in Garage
- Warranty expires in eleven months
- Replacement brushes available
- Manual already attached

Ownership becomes contextual.
